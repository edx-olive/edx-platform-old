Navigating appliedx courses

Click the Course tab to view the course outline page, which lists the sections and sub-sections within the course. Here you can also see which sub-sections have graded quizzes. From the course outline page, you can click any sub-section to begin learning. Sub-sections are composed of units, where each unit is a page within the course. Units consist of various learning blocks such as videos, quiz questions, or text.

Once you’ve clicked into a particular sub-section, click the Next button to navigate to the next unit in the course.

Units within a sub-section are displayed in a navigation bar at the top of the screen. Click “Next” to navigate to the next unit in the course.

Underneath each unit title, you can click to easily find that page in the future. You can access all bookmarked pages by clicking the Course tab and then clicking “Bookmarks” on the right under “Course Tools.”

Video options

When playing videos, you have the following options:

Increase the speed to 1.5x or decrease the speed to 0.75x
Adjust the volume
View full-screen
Turn on/off closed captioning (if captions are available)
Turn on/off transcripts (if transcripts are available)

PATHWAY and AGU credit

To get AGU credit, you will need to get a passing grade for the course. To see what the passing grade is for the course you’re in, click the Progress tab. The passing grade will be displayed on the chart. For most courses, the passing grade is 70%.

On the Progress page, you can also see the sections and sub-sections that contain quizzes. As you complete each quiz, pay attention to the following:

  • Number of attempts: Some quiz questions allow you a limited number of attempts to get the problem right. You will see this next to the “Submit” button.
  • Question type: Checkbox questions will have 1 or more correct answers and have a square next to each answer choice. Multiple-choice questions have only 1 correct answer and have a circle next to each answer choice.
  • Show answer: After submitting your answer, you will be able to select “Show answer” to read additional feedback. Don’t skip these, as they offer valuable insights to aid in your learning.
